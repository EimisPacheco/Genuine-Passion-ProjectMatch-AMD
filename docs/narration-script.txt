Every hiring team is reading the wrong document.

A resume is a sales document — it tells you what a person wants you to believe. But there's another record, and nobody writes it for a recruiter. The repositories they push to at midnight. The hackathon they entered with no prize on the line. The architecture diagram they drew because they couldn't let the problem go.

That record tells you what someone cannot stop building.

You cannot rule-base this. Passion is never declared — it has to be inferred, and half the evidence isn't even text. It's the diagram, the dashboard, the model output.

Why does this matter most for startups? A startup can't outbid Big Tech on salary, and has no brand to trade on. What it can do is find the few engineers already obsessed with its exact problem. The catch is that those people will never apply. They don't know you exist.

And no hiring tool will find them — every one of them only ranks the pile that already applied. If the right person never applied, the tool is structurally blind.

So we don't wait for them. This is Multi-Agent Passion Intelligence. You give it your mission — no names — and it searches GitHub for the builders whose public work already points at what you're building. That's sourcing, not screening.

Then ten agents go to work, coordinated by LangGraph. They read repositories, hackathons, Kaggle and writing. Gemma's vision model reads the architecture diagrams and screenshots. LinkedIn, Medium and lablab.ai sit behind a wall — Bright Data unblocks them. And when someone doesn't list a LinkedIn, we find it the way a recruiter would: a web search, and Gemma verifies it's really them before we trust it. Because a candidate you can't reach is a dead end.

Every score cites a real URL — click any one and it tells you exactly why. Eighteen tests fail the build if anything is invented.

All of it runs on AMD. Gemma 4, thirty-one billion parameters, open weights, on ROCm, on an AMD Instinct MI300X. Reasoning, vision, and embeddings — three modalities, one GPU.

Here's why it should win. It's complete — a live product, not a notebook. It's original — everyone else filters applicants; we find the ones who never applied. And it has a market — every startup is losing the talent war on salary, and this hands them the one advantage money can't buy.

And here's the part I know first-hand. Sometimes the most motivated people in the world are the ones you have to find — the builders who ship at midnight and enter hackathons for the love of it, not to get hired. I'm one of them. I build because I can't not. This is the tool I wish existed: to find people like me, and to let people like me be found for the work they actually do.
